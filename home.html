What is ASD?

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a complex neurodevelopmental condition that affects an individual's social interactions, communication abilities, and behavior. It is referred to as a "spectrum" because it manifests differently in each person, ranging from mild to severe symptoms. People with autism may experience challenges in understanding social cues, engaging in conversations, and forming relationships. Additionally, they may display repetitive behaviors, have intense interests in specific topics, and face difficulties in adapting to changes in their environment or routines. The exact cause of autism is not fully understood, but it is believed to result from a combination of genetic and environmental factors. Early diagnosis and intervention play a crucial role in helping individuals with autism develop essential life skills and improve their quality of life.
